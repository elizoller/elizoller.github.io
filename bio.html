Bio. Eli Scott Zoller.

Want to know a little more about me? You've come to the right place.

I attended University of Mary Washington in Fredericksburg, Virginia from 2008 to 2011 where I earned a Bachelor's in English Literature. I dedicated significant time to math and computer science courses as well. Outside of class, I played rugby (although not very well) and was active in LGBT diversity activities.

After college, I moved to Boston to attend Simmons College starting in fall of 2011. I finished my program in December of 2012, earning my Masters in Library and Information Science in January 2013 with a concentration in Archives. While I was at Simmons, I worked retail and spent many hours cycling around the city of Boston.

I worked at Tufts University during and after grad school at the Hirsh Health Sciences Library as a Library Assistant until I was offered a position for a two-year fellowship for new librarians at the University of Texas at Arlington. In May 2013, I moved myself, my fiance, and our ridiculous cats to Arlington where we live now. My current title is Systems Librarian Fellow for Web and Digital Initiatives.

I'm passionate about trends in technology and web design. I'm a supporter of the open movement whether that be open access or massive open online courses. I like to challenge myself and learn new things all the time.

In my free time, I enjoy long distance bicycling.
